# Final Business Recommendation

## Key Findings

Regression analysis indicates that several operational variables are positively associated with monthly sales.

The strongest predictor identified is Footfall, followed by Inventory Availability and Marketing Spend.



## Recommendations

### Increase Customer Footfall

Invest in marketing campaigns, customer engagement activities, and local events that increase store traffic.

### Maintain High Inventory Availability

Ensure products remain available to reduce missed sales opportunities.

### Continue Targeted Marketing

Marketing spend has a statistically significant positive relationship with sales and should be allocated efficiently.

### Monitor Store Type Performance

Different store types exhibit different sales behaviour. Management should evaluate store-specific strategies rather than applying identical policies across all stores.



## Variables That Should Not Be Over-Interpreted

Regression measures statistical association rather than proving cause-and-effect relationships.

Business decisions should therefore combine regression results with operational knowledge.



## Risks

- Omitted variables may influence sales.
- Seasonal effects were not explicitly modelled.
- Economic conditions and competitor activities are not captured.



## Conclusion

Leadership should prioritise increasing customer footfall, maintaining product availability, and optimising marketing investments. The multiple regression model provides the strongest analytical basis for supporting these business decisions.
