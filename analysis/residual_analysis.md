# Residual Analysis

## Objective

Residual analysis was performed to evaluate the prediction accuracy of the selected multiple regression model.



## Residual Formula

Residual = Actual Monthly Sales − Predicted Monthly Sales

Positive residuals indicate that actual sales were higher than predicted.

Negative residuals indicate that actual sales were lower than predicted.



## Largest Positive Residuals

Stores with the largest positive residuals outperformed the model's expectations.

Possible reasons include:

- Strong local customer loyalty
- Effective local promotions
- Exceptional store management
- Seasonal demand not captured by the model



## Largest Negative Residuals

Stores with the largest negative residuals performed below expectations.

Possible reasons include:

- Local competition
- Temporary operational issues
- Stock shortages
- Regional economic conditions



## Business Interpretation

The model slightly under-predicts performance for certain high-performing stores while over-predicting performance for some lower-performing stores.

Residuals suggest that additional variables such as local demographics, promotional campaigns, and seasonal events may improve future models.



## Conclusion

Overall, the residuals appear reasonably distributed without indicating severe systematic bias. The model provides useful business predictions while acknowledging that not all variation can be explained.
